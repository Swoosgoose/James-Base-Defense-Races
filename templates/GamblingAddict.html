Acquired Message

Something about that game just spoke to you. You NEED more. But where can you get that sort of thrill?

Your eyes seem to remain calm no matter what you face. Almost as if they were dead. That can't be a bad sign.

Template

Description: You seem to have a rotating set of injuries every time someone looks back. Bruised ribs, black eye, etc. People have trouble pinning it down, but something is weird about that.

Weakness:

Addict: The thrill of risk has you in its grasp. You MUST gamble every round of combat involving you or suffer a -4 to all actions.

Losing Streak: If you suffer 3 gambling loses in a row, you may not Cash Out.

Bad Luck: When gambled bennies are lost they become bad luck. This does not stack with other race's bad luck

Powers:

Winner's High: Upon successfully gambling, you gain a +2 to your next action

Cash Out: You may trade 10 bennies to "save 1 for next session". Maximum "savable" bennies is equal to your "refresh" bennies.

Hard to Read: Gain a +1 when trying to overstate the strength of your "hand".

Gambling Addict Edges

Dealt, The Hand You're

Requirements: Acquired Race: Gambling Addict, Novice

When the cards are down and you must act you find that some luck may come your way. You may gamble on action cards dealt to you before the start of a round.

Team Counting

Requirements: Acquired Race: Gambling Addict, Veteran, The Hand You're Dealt

You can use your team members to offset your poor preparation and luck. Once cards have been dealt you may trade with a team member (they must agree) swapping your initiatives. If this card causes you to win a bet you get Winner's High and do not lose any bennies. However you do not gain the bennies for winning the bet.

Card Counting Team

Requirements: Acquired Race: Gambling Addict, Heroic, Team Counting

When using Team Count to swap cards with a team member, if you win a bet also gain 1/2 earnings rounded up.

Playing it Safe

Requirements: Acquired Race: Gambling Addict, Novice, The Hand You're Dealt

Luck comes in colors and you just need to pick your lucky color to win. There is safety in a little bit of certainty. You can bet on color with a bet of 0. Every 2 you get right in a session gets you another benny. Losing any bets resets this count.

High-Low

Requirements: Acquired Race: Gambling Addict, Seasoned, The Hand You're Dealt

You can feel opposing tugs of others fate against your own. The more that oppose you the tougher the odds and greater the payout. Declare whether your card will be higher or lower than the opposing characters. Gain bennies equal to number of bennies bet times half number of opposing characters rounded down.

Dealt, Winning Hand

Requirements: Acquired Race: Gambling Addict, Heroic, High-Low

While some fates oppose you, you can find those whose align to help resist. You team up with as many people as oppose you to pool cards. Hands are limited to 5 cards. You may gamble bennies, but do not have to. This counts as a gamble either way. All cards not on a team are "board" cards. Loosing team takes a -1 to all actions for the round. Winning team gains a +1 to all actions for the round.

Striking it Rich

Requirements: Acquired Race: Gambling Addict, Legendary, Winning Hand Dealt

Fate is turbulent with you around. Large swings are bound to happen. When using winning hand, winners gain 1 benny for each level higher their hand was than their opponents. These bennies can be split however the team wants.
Example 1: You both have a straight, but yours is higher. No additional bennies are handed out.
Example 2: Your team has 4 of a kind. Enemies have a pair. 6 bennies are handed out and your team may split them how you wish.

Crushing Defeat

Requirements: Acquired Race: Gambling Addict, Legendary, Winning Hand Dealt

Those who are less prepared for the swings of fate suffer the most. Losing team now takes a -2 to all actions for the round.

Proxy

Requirements: Acquired Race: Gambling Addict, Veteran, Streetwise d10

Sometimes you need someone to act on your behalf. There are odd restrictions but they are not enough to prevent this. You may select another wild card you are allied with at the start of the time period who you can contact. This allows you to use your abilities, bennies, and gambles as if they were you. This also means that, if they enter combat, you must gamble every round or you both take a penalty to all actions.

Proxy, Standard

Requirements: Acquired Race: Gambling Addict, Heroic, Proxy, Streetwise d12

The unreasonable, arbitary requirement is mostly gone. You may select non-wild card characters to act as your proxy. They must meet all the same requirements as a wild card.

Just Once More

Requirements: Acquired Race: Gambling Addict, Novice, Gambling d6

Your able to gamble through dark patches. Increase loses required to trigger Losing Streak by 1. This can be taken twice.

Picking Up by Bootstraps

Requirements: Acquired Race: Gambling Addict, Seasoned, Gambling d10

When the world seems against you, you can still prepare for the future. You can Cash Out once (more) after a losing streak.

Out but not Down

Requirements: Acquired Race: Gambling Addict, Heroic, Healing d6, Gambling d10

You gamble your health away in hopes of luck. You may use wounds as bennies for gambles.

Gamble, Impromptu

Requirements: Acquired Race: Gambling Addict, Novice, Gambling d4

Challenging others appears to affect their luck too. Trigger a roll off against your luck (wild die ONLY) resisted by the targets spirit. On success roll on the earnings table for the target.

Gamble, Targeted

Requirements: Acquired Race: Gambling Addict, Seasoned, Impromptu Gamble, Gambling d8

When using Impromptu Gamble, if you raise your luck check you can chose the top or bottom of the table to roll against.

Gamble, Defense

Requirements: Acquired Race: Gambling Addict, Veteran, Targeted Gamble

Things often get in between you and your enemies, not that that is a bad thing. You may spend a benny to roll luck (wild di[c]e) for your parry.

Gamble, Offensive

Requirements: Acquired Race: Gambling Addict, Heroic, Defense Gamble

Your enemies seem to trip on themselves, get crushed by falling objects, and spontaneously combust more often than normal. You may spend a benny to add luck (wild di[c]e) to a damage roll.

If I had a Nickel

Requirements: Acquired Race: Gambling Addict, Legendary, Spirit d8

Stuff happens in sets and that is good for you. Select a number at the start of a time period. Every time after the first that number is rolled during a time period gain a benny. This is not a gamble.

I'd Have Two Nickels

Requirements: Acquired Race: Gambling Addict, Legendary, If I had a Nickel

If the number appeared exactly once last time period you can use that number in the next time period as one of your results.

Bad Luck

Bad Luck is like a GM benny, but it can only be used against you. In addition they can pool to create more powerful effects (GM’s discretion). Bad Luck builds over time and does not go away until it is used (does not disappear after session).

Max Luck

New cap for bennies in a session 20x "refresh" bennies... this may be a bad idea, we will see. This is true for all of them. (Note this is raising it from the previous 5x)
